# Design Lore — Psychology

Advanced UX psychology module for deep theoretical analysis. Use when the user
requests psychological frameworks, theory-led audits, archetype analysis,
persuasion evaluation, or asks about specific theorists.

> **Part of the Design Lore family.** This module provides the theoretical
> depth layer. For core Figma and design knowledge, use **design-lore**.
> For ecommerce and crypto/fintech UX, use **design-lore-industry**.

## How to Use This Skill

1. Identify which theoretical school is most relevant to the question
2. Read the applicable entries from the module
3. Apply theories as analytical lenses, not prescriptive rules
4. For comprehensive audits, combine multiple schools (see dp-027, dp-036)

## Key Principles

- **Know your persuasion level**: Every design choice operates on the ethics spectrum — from education to coercion. Know where you are.
- **Design for the activity, not the click**: Understand the motive (Leontiev), not just the operation.
- **Theory is a lens, not a prescription**: Use psychological frameworks to analyse and illuminate, not to dictate.
- **The Shadow is real**: Every product has unconscious effects on users — acknowledge and design for them.
- **Invisible is the goal**: The best interfaces disappear (Heidegger's ready-to-hand). When users notice the tool, something broke.
